Team Stella's Design Intelligence ™ (DI) framework brings together User-Centered Design (UCD) and Agile methods, seamlessly integrating a humanistic way of thinking into our delivery approach. UCD is crucial to staying close and connected to project stakeholders, users, and organizational leadership to ensure the teams are collaborating effectively. Team Steampunk’s service, user-experience (UX), and product designers work closely with all these groups to ensure all involved parties’ needs are represented, that all stakeholders and users are aligned on the problem(s) being solved, and that user stories properly capture the requirements and priorities.

Our designers do far more than “make things pretty”. Stella’s Design & Strategy practice is made up of a wide variety of design disciplines including service designers, design-strategists, UX designers, content strategists, interaction designers, and visual designers. Our designers are embedded members of our Agile team and are involved from discovery to deployment, not just conducting research in the beginning and then passing it over for others to interpret and incorporate. In order to maximize team members’ contributions, Team Stella uses designers with expertise in more than one design discipline such as service design and user experience design or experience design and interaction design—meeting SPEED requirements for multi-skilled, “T-shaped” team members who have a deep understanding within a focused discipline and a broad understand of a multitude of others.

Within our DI framework, our designers use a series of UCD methods throughout delivery, alongside our Agile and DevSecOps-focused work. By seamlessly integrating Agile ceremonies with our user-centric methods, we significantly strengthen our Agile delivery, ensuring users and stakeholders are a part of the creation process vs. being passive observers. The goal of our team’s design activities is to understand who the users and stakeholders are as people (thinking, feeling, seeing, doing); their relationships between each other, their processes, and the systems they interact with; what their current state is and how they might be impacted by change; the challenges they face and what they need; the goals of features and functions and how they can be designed and presented simply and intuitively; and how the experience can be continuously tested and developed. DI facilitates the integration of this deep understanding into the team’s Agile planning activities and ceremonies so we can improve the overall, end to end relationship people have with the services and products they use, increasing adoption by making them usable and accessible, and establishing trust by giving them what they told us they need. **Team Stella’s integration of UCD into our Agile and DevSecOps delivery represents a significant strength of our overall development approach that puts customers at the heart of everything we do to drive the business value they need and in turn achieve the customer satisfaction OCIO desires.**

For this Code Challenge we applied several design techniques using templates we have built to provide a consistent approach to Design Intelligence &trade;.

## User Research

### Research Plan

This research plan articulates the objectives, methodology, participants and recruitment strategy, and Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) to be measured to understand the current state of the I-131 experience. This plan guided the interviews that ultimately informed the UCD artifacts (such as user personas, journey maps, service blueprints, wireframes), which ensure that the digitized experience filing, submitting, and adjudicating the I-131 form caters to the real needs of users.

Benefit: Articulate the objectives and strategy for conducting user research. Serve as a guide for how research activities will serve the development of the solution

### Empathy Maps 

Empathy maps are a method for understanding a group of users, that breaks down what they are *thinking or feeling*, what their *goals and objectives* are, and what are their *needs and pain points*. These are the foundations for what will become the user personas.

Benefit: Gain insight into users to understand them as humans, ensuring a solution that is both functional and desirable
* [Code Challenge I-131 Empathy Map](Image location)
* [Empathy Map Template](Image location)

### User Interview

Benefit: Gain an understanding of people and their views of the world by eliciting true feelings, desires, struggles and opinions through a few carefully crafted questions. Allows for illuminating discoveries that have large impact on product and feature planning

### User Personas

User personas represent the archetypes of users who may interact 
with a system or process, built out of the data from user interviews. Personas
include details such as demographics and psychographics, habits and behaviors,
needs and objectives. They grounded our ideation in empathy, ensuring that 
features and solutions meet the needs of the users.

Benefit: Create archetype for the types of users who the solution was built for, understanding the people, sytstems, and touchpoints that they interact with

## Product Design

### Card Sort

In order to create a truly digital experience (rather than just putting the I-131 form online), we conducted card sorting exercise to understand the information architecture, categorization, and prioritization of content. This involved breaking down the components of the I-131 form, viewing it from an applicant's perspective, and re-arranging it in a way that would result in the best possible experience. 

Benefit: Ensure that the solution delivers an experience that reflects a logical flow of information and content 

* [Code Challenge I-131 Card Sort](Image location)

### Style Guide

We incorporate responsive design best practices, the [United States Web Design System](https://designsystem.digital.gov/) (USWDS) including 508 compliant components, interaction patterns, and templates to make sure the interface is not only accessible and available on any device but also visually consistent throughout the larger mission ecosystem.

Benefit: Dictate the visual design, interaction patterns, and accessibility of the solution to ensure it is consistent across all components as well as with other systems

* [Visual Design Style Guide](Image location)

## Usability Testing 

Usability tests are a method for understanding how users will actually 
interact with the solution. These are conducted by having users from 
different persona types test the prototype and think aloud, discussing
their thought process, raise questions, and voice concerns about their 
experience.

Benefit: Ensure that the solution being built matches with the needs and behaviors of users and that the system delivers a positive experience
